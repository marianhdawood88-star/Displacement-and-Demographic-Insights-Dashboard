# Displacement and Demographic Insights Dashboard

## Overview
[cite_start]This project provides a comprehensive data-driven dashboard designed to monitor and analyze the living conditions and demographic distribution of displaced populations across Syria, Yemen, Iraq, and Lybia[cite: 1, 2, 4, 6]. [cite_start]By centralizing key humanitarian metrics, this dashboard assists stakeholders in assessing needs, optimizing resource allocation, and tracking the welfare of these populations[cite: 3, 9, 28].

## Key Features
* [cite_start]**Humanitarian Impact Tracking:** Monitors the number of families currently residing in tents by country[cite: 3].
* [cite_start]**Geographic Analysis:** Tracks the scale of displacement across various cities in the region[cite: 9].
* [cite_start]**Demographic Breakdown:** Detailed insights into total family numbers (1,701), total members (11,905), and specific vulnerable populations, including 2,695 females under 18 and 3,999 members of mother-headed households[cite: 20, 22, 23, 25, 26, 27].
* [cite_start]**Economic Indicators:** Analyzes total income (58,385) and average income (85.61) to provide context on the economic stability of displaced communities[cite: 21, 24].
* [cite_start]**Gender Metrics:** Provides a breakdown of population gender ratios, identifying 45% male and 55% female distribution[cite: 36, 37, 38].

## Data Summary
The following data points are central to the analysis provided by this dashboard:
* [cite_start]**Total Families:** 1,701 [cite: 20]
* [cite_start]**Total Members:** 11,905 [cite: 23]
* [cite_start]**Total Income:** 58,385 [cite: 21]
* [cite_start]**Average Income:** 85.61 [cite: 24]
* [cite_start]**Gender Ratio:** 45% Male, 55% Female [cite: 37, 38]

## Visualization
The dashboard utilizes charts to represent critical data, including:
* [cite_start]**Families in Tents by Country:** Visual comparison of displaced family counts in Syria (138), Yemen (120), Iraq (45), and Lybia (41)[cite: 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 17, 19].
* [cite_start]**City Count by Country:** Tracking the number of affected cities across the monitored countries, with 6 cities in Syria, 5 in Yemen, 4 in Iraq, and 4 in Lybia[cite: 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16].
